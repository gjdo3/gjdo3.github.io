Nicolas Jaar - Pomegranates

I uh, have been having a lot of trouble trying to articulate what I think about this soundtrack. I haven't watched Pomegranates, so I don't know if the music is fitting to the tone of the movie. It sounds eerie, lonely, and sad. It sounds like the soundtrack to a walk through an empty town, or an unfamiliar city. It's like walking along the sea when the fog is rolling in, and you look up and down the beach, and you are alone.

Pomegranates, the soundtrack, opens up with Garden of Eden, a noisy, static filled track similar to Jaar's earlier work in the Darkside duo Golden Arrow. Construction starts off with the sound of rustle papers and heavy things being moved in the distance. Maybe flowing water? It's hard to tell. The ambient noises are distorted and mixed back into the trick. Eventually a lonely piano starts playing, but that only shows how desolate the "music" sounds, how empty the soundscape Jaar creates is, like a metronome counting in a quiet room.

Now I'm not gonna go and do a track by track review, I just want to establish how the album opens up. Starting on the third track, Pass The Time, I'm getting a UK Garage vibe, and the album starts to resemble more standard electronic music. I say resemble, because it never sounds like something you would find on a house or techno label. The music stays distorted, it has an almost industrial quality too it. Along with synths, Jaar is using instrumental samples (whether recorded in studio, or taken from pre existing music, I do not know.) to keep things interested. You can hear down pitched bells, plucked strings, piano and organ throughout, along with the occasional chopped up vocal sample.

Divorce and Muse stand out by being the most conventional tracks out of the lot, both being incredibly sad piano ballads.

I did really enjoy this release, and at the end I found myself wanting more, despite it being ~80 minutes long. If you're in the mood to brood, I can readily recommend this to you

Favorite track: Muse

Least Favorite Track: Screams At The Edge Of Dawn
