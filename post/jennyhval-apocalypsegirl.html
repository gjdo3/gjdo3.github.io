Jenny Hval

Apocalypse, girl

This album feels very personal, to the point where I was uncomfortable while listening to it. Jenny Hval talks about things that, to me, seem very personal. I felt like she was writing in her diary and I was looking over her shoulder. You see a lot of pop music talk about sex and relationships, but I have rarely seen it handled on such a personal level.

This isn't your run of the mill singer-songwriter album. This isn't one woman singing away on an acoustic guitar or ukulele. The arrangements here are dark, foreboding. I think this album would work very well as a dark ambient album if you removed the vocals. Not that the vocals make it any less uncomfortable to listen to, for me anyway.

I actually felt like a voyeur while listening to this. I don't know if that was the intention, but I don't usually like singer-songwriters, so this album was refreshing for how different it is.

Favorite track: Holy Land

Least Favorite Track: Some Days
