Earl Sweatshirt

I Don't Like Shit, I Don't Go Outside

Now this is an album title after my own heart. I see it and I think to myself, well, I don't like a lot of things, and I don't go outside all that much, so this must be for me!

In reality, I am not a fan of Earl Sweatshirt. I can understand his appeal as a lyricist, but I his flat, uninterested, and slow flow, combined with the trap-rap production on a lot of his songs make for a perfect storm of "not for me." However, I heard that this album was different than Earl's earlier work, and so I gave it a shot. I'm left feeling pleasantly surprised. I wouldn't say I "like" this album, but I did like it more than Doris. If doom-rap or funeral-hop becomes a genre, this will be its' "Epicus Doomicus Metallicus".

I'm still not sold on Earl's flow, but the relentlessly dreary and dark production on this album really compliment his droning, deadened delivery. If cloud rap is dream-pop, this is doom-drone, as far as atmosphere goes. The beats are slow and heavy, and the whole album sounds fuzzy and blown out.

Like I said earlier I still don't really like Earl Sweatshirt, or this album for that matter, but I am interested in where he is going to go from here, and I'll be sure to check out his new release, whenever they come.

Favorite track: Huey

Least Favorite Track: Grief
