Yelawolf - Love Story

If Kid Rock and Eminem did collaboration I think it would sound like this album. I actually find some of the beats on this thing pretty interesting, if not good. I enjoy the country flavor a lot of these tracks have. When I first gave this a listen I didn't know that Yelawolf was on Shady Records but I felt a very strong Eminem influence. This hunch turned out to be well founded after some research. Now to clarify, I haven't been a fan of any of Eminem’s more recent albums, actually, I can't say I "liked" an Eminem record since "Encore" came out and that album has lost its appeal. I say this because, while I do get an Eminem vibe out of this album, it's "new" Eminem. More songs feature Yelawolf actually singing instead of rapping and, to me, they sound like they are going for pop appeal with would-be catchy choruses.

I don't think this album is bad, but I don't think it is good either. I think that this record suffered from not being weird enough. If Yelawolf had gone fully into making a country/bluegrass-hip-hop fusion, I would have enjoyed this more for the sheer novelty of it. As it is, it's passable. I probably won't be going back to this thing anytime soon but I don't regret checking it out.

Favorite track:Till It's Gone

Least Favorite Track:Best Friend
