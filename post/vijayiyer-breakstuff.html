Vijay Iyer Trio - Break Stuff

This is the kind of music I could see being played at the Village Vanguard. There's a subtlety to the music here that takes some attention to appreciate. Vijay Iyer's classic jazz trio put out some impressive work on here for how it manages to stay interesting despite how lengthy some of the pieces get. It's improv without being masturbatory. There's a cohesiveness to the music, despite it being a collection of Iyer's own work and covers, which I think come from how "together" the band is, how quickly it adapts to changes in whatever is being improvised.

However, just because it doesn't get boring doesn't mean it stays distinct. My biggest complaint about this album is how all the individual tracks seem to bleed into each other, stylistically. If I'm not paying attention, it becomes hard to tell when one ends and another begins, and this isn't a live album, as far as I know. I think that in trying to make a record with the energy and uniqueness characteristic of breaks in music, the whole album ended up sounding same-y.

Favorite track: Countdown

Least Favorite Track: Hood
